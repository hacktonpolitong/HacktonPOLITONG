SB-Inspect 200 - Autonomous Inventory Scanning Robot

SB-Inspect 200 helps warehouse teams verify inventory position, label readability, and stock-location accuracy without adding manual cycle-counting workload during peak shifts.

Designed use cases:

- retail distribution centers with many SKUs;
- fashion and footwear warehouses;
- pharma and healthcare logistics warehouses requiring careful stock verification;
- spare-parts warehouses;
- manufacturing warehouses with component storage and line-side replenishment areas.

What the system does:

- scans barcode labels, bin locations, and selected pallet/tote labels;
- drives through mapped aisles during low-traffic windows;
- compares scan output against a CSV or WMS inventory export;
- flags missing labels, misplaced items, unreadable labels, and location mismatches;
- creates a supervisor review report before any official stock update.

Pilot positioning for Italy:

The first Italian pilot should be limited to one aisle group, one SKU family, or one warehouse zone. The recommended pilot output is a report-only exception list, not an automatic change to the buyer's stock records. This lowers IT and operations risk while allowing the buyer to measure scan coverage, inventory accuracy, cycle-counting time, and exception resolution effort.

Buyer roles to involve:

- Logistics Director;
- Warehouse Manager;
- Inventory Control Manager;
- IT/WMS Lead;
- Quality Manager for regulated warehouses.

Buyer concerns to prepare for:

- CE/safety evidence for operating near people and goods;
- data security review for WMS export handling;
- Wi-Fi and barcode readability;
- local maintenance and spare-part support;
- proof from similar warehouse environments;
- ROI model based on the buyer's own cycle-counting baseline.
