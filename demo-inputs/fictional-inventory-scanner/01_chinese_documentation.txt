Internal Product Documentation Summary

Vendor: Hangzhou ScanBridge Robotics
Product: SB-Inspect 200 Inventory Scanning Robot
Product category: inventory scanning robot
Target market for this test: Italy

SB-Inspect 200 is an autonomous mobile scanning robot designed for warehouse inventory checks. The system is intended for high-SKU retail distribution centers, pharma logistics warehouses, spare-parts warehouses, and industrial distribution sites where inventory accuracy and cycle-counting workload are recurring operational problems.

The robot navigates a mapped warehouse aisle group and scans barcode shelf labels, bin locations, tote labels, and selected pallet labels. It then compares the scan result against a WMS export file and creates an exception report. The first pilot should be report-only: the robot should not automatically update official inventory records until the Italian buyer validates exception logic and data quality.

Main operating mode:

- map one aisle group or one SKU family;
- run during night shift or low-traffic windows;
- scan barcode labels and shelf/bin positions;
- compare scan results against CSV inventory export;
- create exception list for buyer review;
- review exceptions with warehouse supervisor before updating official records.

Operational value:

- improve scan coverage in selected aisles;
- reduce manual cycle-counting hours;
- detect missing, misplaced, duplicate, or unreadable labels;
- produce a repeatable inventory-control workflow;
- support a low-risk pilot because the first output is a report, not an automatic stock correction.

Current limitations:

- barcode labels must be visible and readable;
- Wi-Fi coverage is required along the mapped aisle group;
- reflective packaging and poor label placement can reduce scan quality;
- the buyer must provide a WMS inventory export or CSV file;
- the robot needs a safe route and pedestrian interaction review;
- official inventory records should not be updated automatically during the pilot.
