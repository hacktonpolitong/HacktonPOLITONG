Case Study, ROI, And Deployment Notes

Fictional Chinese demo context:

Hangzhou ScanBridge Robotics tested SB-Inspect 200 in a fictional Chinese retail warehouse environment. The robot was used for night-shift scan coverage across selected high-SKU aisles. The output was an exception list reviewed by warehouse supervisors. No automatic WMS stock updates were enabled during the first test.

Transferable pilot logic:

The Italian pilot should not promise the same result as the Chinese demo. It should use the buyer's own baseline and measure whether a report-only scanning workflow improves inventory-control visibility.

Recommended Italian pilot:

- duration: 30 days;
- scope: one aisle group or one SKU category;
- systems: one SB-Inspect 200 robot;
- operating window: night shift or low-traffic scanning windows;
- data mode: CSV inventory export, report-only exception output;
- buyer review: supervisor checks exceptions before any stock-record update;
- fallback: manual cycle counting remains active.

Pilot KPIs:

- scan coverage rate in the selected aisle group;
- inventory accuracy delta against buyer baseline;
- manual cycle-counting hours reduced;
- exception resolution time;
- unreadable label rate;
- data sync success rate.

ROI notes:

No localized Italian ROI model is available yet. The pilot should collect the buyer's current cycle-counting hours, exception rate, rework effort, and stock-adjustment process. Any ROI argument should be based on the buyer's own baseline after the pilot, not on a generic claim.

Deployment risks:

- poor barcode label visibility;
- Wi-Fi gaps in warehouse aisles;
- unclear data security expectations from IT;
- safety review needed before robot enters live warehouse aisles;
- no local maintenance partner currently identified;
- no Italian reference customer.

Recommended risk reducers:

- report-only pilot;
- one aisle group only;
- low-traffic scanning windows;
- manual cycle count remains available;
- weekly review with inventory supervisor and IT/WMS lead;
- exit condition after 30 days if scan quality or support expectations are not met.
